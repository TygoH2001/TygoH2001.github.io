1) How many of the canal boats currently in use are diesel/fossil fuel driven and how many boats are electrical driven?

Amsterdam wants to ban diesel engines from the centre's canals by 2025 completely. In 2022, approximately 33% of the 7350 registered vessels in Amsterdam were already fully or partly emission-free. That is a total of 2453 boats. Note: hybrid boats are only allowed to use their electric motor inside Amsterdam’s city center and are therefore considered electrically driven for this calculation. 


      # How much is 33% of the 7350 registered vessels?
      
      number_boats = 7350
      percentage_electric = 33
      
      electric_boats = (percentage_electric / 100) * number_boats

Try to compare the energy use of the canal boats to some other activities in the city. Use canal boats more or less energy in relation to their carbon footprint compared to these other activities?

It is very difficult to compare the energy use of electric boats to other activities because there is not much data publicly available. Also, the periods of use, purposes of use and boat models differ a lot. We found that the CO2 emissions of canal boats in Amsterdam are included within inland shipping numbers. Inland shipping is responsible for 2% of the CO2 emissions of the Mobility & Transport sector. This is not a lot compared to road traffic emissions. Assuming the average person spends 50 hours per year on their boat (with a 50-horsepower motor), they use 175kWh – 236 kWh energy per year. This would produce 0.076 metric tons C02 equivalent.
